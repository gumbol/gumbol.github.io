Intro to favicons

So what is a favicon and what is it used for?

A favicon is a 32x32 or even 16x16 pixel image that appears on the browser tabs, but also in other places like bookmarks, search engine results... etc.

From its name, its purpose is to act as an icon to the favored/bookmarked links.

So can these tiny images do much?

It depends, if you browse your favorite/bookmarked pages you usually see a list of page titles. What a favicon image does is show the source of the pages visually. It also keeps your users brand exposoure consistent. This can be significant if you are building a brand for example.

Favicons can be useful in search engines results. Desgining an attrictive favicon can help encourage users to click on your page links.

Favicon design

If you look at the icon examples above you'd notice icons that are alphanumeral (like the W3) or a mascot (like GIMP) The colors are also not more than 2 (at least the major ones)

This makes favicons easier to recognize. Adding more colors can make it hard for users to recognize the brand/logo.

Favicons dont have to be 2d either. They can be 3d, or even animated.
