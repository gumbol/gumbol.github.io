Find full length music on Internet Archive

When you search the Archive.org for music the results you get are usually sample music. If you needed to listen to full length music like i did you need to create some special queries.

Creative Commons

By including a creative common (CC) license metadata in the search query you will get full length music in most cases.

Types of creative common licenses

There are 6 CC license that can be used

by (Attribution only) The default setting and least restrictive

by-sa (Attribution and share alike)

by-nd (Attribution and no derivatives)

by-nc (Attribution and non-commercial)

by-nc-sa (Attribution, non-commercial and share alike)

by-nc-nd (Attribution, non-commercial and no derivatives)

Once you pick the licenses you like you need to include it in the search query. The syntax for that query would looks something like this:

[<SEARCH TERM>][licenseurl:http*<LICENSE TYPE>*] Example: jazz licenseurl:http*by*

To include more than one license type you separate them with "OR" operator

Example: jazz licenseurl:http*by-nc OR by-nd*

Conclusion

The internet archive has a great deal of content that without a good grasp of query syntax it would be diffcult to find what you want. Now that you have some basic understanding of how to query using the CC syntax I encourage you to learn more about internet archive query syntax and operations. I hope you found this article helpful.
