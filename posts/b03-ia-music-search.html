When i search the Archive.org for music the results i get are almost always sample music.

I needed to listen to full length music so i did some research and found out a neat solution.

By including the CreativeCommon (CC) license metadata in the search query I always get full length music. But how to search for it you might ask? The first thing i did was to take a look at the CC license types.

There are 6 CC license types

  • by
  • by-sa
  • by-nd
  • by-nc
  • by-nc-sa
  • by-nc-nd

The syntax for the search query looks like this

[<SEARCH TERM>][licenseurl:http*<LICENSE TYPE>*]

You can use "by" license as a wild card to search for music that has any of the license types. Or you can use a more specific type. You can also include more than one type in the search query.

Example:

jazz licenseurl:http*by*

To include more than one license type you separate them with "OR" operator

Example:

jazz licenseurl:http*by-nc OR by-nd*

I tried all the CC types and the results were full length music!.
