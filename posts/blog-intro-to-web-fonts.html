Intro to web fonts

Generic font families

There are 5 generic font families. Each family is a collection of fonts with similar stylistic form.

The examples are text and not images. Meaning you wont see a difference in font style if your system does not have that font family or font type installed.

Serif

Serif typefaces have decorative slab-like appendages (serifs) on the ends of certain letter strokes. Example: ABC abc 123

Sans-serif

Sans-serif typefaces have straight letter strokes that do not end in serifs. Example: ABC abc 123

Monospace

In Monospace typefaces all characters take up the same amount of space on a line. For example, a capital M will be no wider than a lowercase i. Example: ABC abc 123

Cursive

Cursive fonts emulate a script or handwritten style. Example: ABC abc 123

Fantasy

Fantasy fonts are more decorative and would be appropriate for headlines and other display type. Fantasy fonts are rarely used for web text due to cross-platform availability and legibility. Example: ABC abc 123

The most used font on the web is Arial. Followed by "Times Roman". Research showed that these are the most readable font type for the web.
