When i had to buy a display monitor i was confused (still do) by the many different factors that goes into image display. But after researching it turns out that most of the info are markting. A display monitor for gaming (or any really) needs the following things:

  • The width and length of an image (resolution)
  • The accuracy of the image (#pixels per inch)
  • The smoothnes of the image (#frames per second)

Note

You probably heard of Aspect ratio. This ratio is arbitrary; It has no direct affect on the display rendering of an image. The only time you should consider aspect ratio is if you had to work with media of different aspect ratio than the display native. If that is the case the image would need to be stretched, cropped or padded with blank sides to fill the screen.

You probably saw that 16:9 aspect ratio is advertised alot. This is because currently many people use this ratio when they produce media. In the future there might be a different popular ratio.

Resolution

Resolution is the amout of pixels there are in the screen. but alone it does not show how accurate an image can be. that is why pixels are mapped into inches to give it a physical dimension. In other words we can say that 1 inch has x amount of pixels. That is why we need PPI

Pixels per inch

What determine an image quality is the pixel density. This goes hand in hand with the resolution number since A good number is 110-120ppi To save time i listed the numbers for the 27' and 32' display monitors
27' Display should support a resolution of
2560x1440 -> 108.79ppi

32' Display should support a resolution of
3200x1800 -> 114.73ppi
3840x2160 -> 137.68ppi

Frame rate

This is the amount of images displayed per second. A good range is 60-126Mhz
