Intro to UX design

Despite the infitie ways you can "style" a website. There are instrinsic limitations that you need to be aware of. One of you compare a website to One of the limiting factors of websites is that there is little space to work with. That means there is little space to display/explain/demonstrait whatever your product or service your website provide. This is specially true for mobile screens. Another factor is that you cannot engage with content with all your senses. you cannot smell things you cannot feel things, you cannot taste things. You cannot feel things. even special experience is limited in a 2d space (although VR might change that soon). nonetheless, you have 2 senses to work with to form the user experience. Visual and Auditory. Jesse James Garet author of the book the elements of style has applied website design to a spectrum. It is between a Library and a Gallery. In one extreme you have pure functionality and no stimulation. and in the other extreme is pure graphical website with little usability. I think this also applies to working with the users senses. Too much stimulation without inviting the other senses makes the experience incomplete. Also too much stimulation makes the experience annoying. We have to work with a small window in a way to present as much information as possible that can fit into that window without comprimising usability or style. Striking the middle ground can be easy. But you also need to keep people coming back. Good functionality will keep users coming but coupled with balnd style might make users less likely to come back as much. So with the infinite ways to design a website. You will only work with 2 senses or none in the case of blind or deaf users. With that settled now we need to know how to work with these two senses to provide a great user experience. Most blogs and in fact any website is comprised of text. Not only is text used for content value but also to rank up in search engines as web crawlers use text and not images to understand the content of a web page.
