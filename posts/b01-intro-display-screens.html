Intro to display screens

A display screen can be broken into two functions Frames per second (FPS) and Resolution

Frames per second (FPS)

Screens display images as a series of frames. A single picture is 1 frame. More frames per second (FPS) translate to a clearer picture or a smoother motion in the case of motion pictures.

Screens varie in the amount of FPS. For example a monitor ranges from 32 to 126 FPS. A smarphone screen would have different ranges

Resolution

Resolution is the amount of pixels on a screen. More pixels translate to a crisper or more detailed image. Pixels has no physical measurement and therefore an image pixel density will be different in different screen sizes.

Pixels are usually mapped to inches so for example a tablet screen and smarphone screen have different amount of PPI (pixel per inch) so a ppi in one screen can be differnt in another.
