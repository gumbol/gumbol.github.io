How Display monitors work

A Display monitor can be broken into three functions

  • Frames per second
  • Resolution
  • Dimension

Frames per second (FPS)

Motion pictures are a series of pictures or images that collectevly create the illusion of movment. a single picture is 1 frame. More frames per second translate to smoother motion.

What FPS to go with?

Monitors support varies. It ranges from 32 to 126 FPS and its measured in MHz. A good FPS target would be 32.

Resolution

Resolution is the amount of pixels on a screen. Pixels are also the unit used to measure an image dimensions in the digital realm since they have no physical reference. Think how you zoom on an image. this "zoom" factor varies between monitors. so 1 pixel 1 one monitor is 2 in another. that is why pixels are usually mapped to inches to give it a physical dimension.

Aspect ratio: is the relation between an image height and width. Key points about aspect ratio:

The image will always be distorted in some way if the image aspect ratio of the image different from the screen's native aspect ratio or if you edited the image into a different aspect ratio.

Example: Original picture 800x600 (4:3)

Resized to 500x375 (4:3) the image maintains the same look.

Resized to 300x600 (1:2) you can see how the image got stretched out.

To maintain the aspect ratio of an image, screens can be configured to keep the original aspect ratio and fill out the extra space with black pillars on the sides or above and below the image.

Example: Original picture 800x600 (4:3)

Original picture 800x600 (4:3) adjusted to fit in a 16:9 aspect ratio.

What aspect ratio to get?

When buying a monitor make sure it support a native aspect ratio that is widely used. for example a 16:9 aspect ratio is currently a popular aspect ratio used for all sort of media content productions.

Dimension

What determine an image accuracy is the pixel density. more pixels per inch means more image accuracy. This goes hand in hand with the dimension of the monitor. A good pixel per inch (ppi) number is between 110-120.

Below is the resolution and ppi numbers recommended for common monitor sizes.

  • 27' (diagonal) Display should support at least a resolution of 2560x1440 which gives 108.79ppi
  • 32' (diagonal) Display should support at least resolution of 3200x1800 which gives 114.73ppi.

If you want a different monitor size then you might want to use a ppi calculator
