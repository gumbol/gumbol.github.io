Intro to display screens

A display screen can be broken into three functions Frames per second (FPS) and Resolution

Frames per second (FPS)

screens display images in a series of frames. A single picture is 1 frame. More frames per second translate to a clearer picture or a smoother motion in the case of movies.

Screens varie in the amount of FPS. For example a monitor ranges from 32 to 126 FPS. A smarphone screen would have different ranges

Resolution

Resolution is the amount of pixels on a screen. Pixels are also the unit used to measure an image dimensions in the digital realm since they have no physical reference. For example a tablet screen and smarphone screen. Each one has different amount of PPI (pixel per inch) so 1 pixel in one screen can be 2 in another. Pixels are usually mapped to inches so it can be referenced in the physical dimension.
